Mobile Technology Trends

The number of mobile devices is significantly increasing. The statistics report published by Federica Laricchia in Mar, 2023 says it could be up to 18.22 billion by 2025 from 2020 for 4.2 billion [1]. The variety of usage has been widespread, especially in AI development. Not only AI, Augmented Reality (AR) has come into place. It uses mobile hardware to generate and process images. An example of using AR technology on mobile devices could be PokemonGo [2], which uses the camera and the mobile processors' power to virtualize the characters into the real world. It means that the software and hardware must be improved by optimizing the operating system allowing AI to use the device's resources efficiently, and developing new tools and SDKs (Software Development Kits) that facilitate the AI development.

In addition, many smartphone brands have started integrating engines or chips for machine learning. Huawei put the first Neural Engine into their smartphones last year, called Kirin processor / 970 chipset [3]. Moreover, Apple has put AI processors into the A12 bionic chip, making AI task processing more efficient. They are investing in user experience in AI on mobile devices by improving performance and battery efficiency. The trend is potentially the subsequent technology in the next 5 - 10 years.

As you can see, the developer with skill sets in mobile development, either native or cross-platform, combined with the machine learning skill set, would easily ride on the trend. Using ML and AI to help us solve the problem will also be a solution to getting on the train. The legacy programming languages such as Objective-C and Java (possibly) for mobile development could get abandoned because of the reduction of community. The Swift programming language (backed by Apple) [4] could replace Objective-C, and Kotlin could replace Java (backed by Google) [5]. Mixing modern high-level programming languages and knowledge of AI and ML could become a way to speak about job-related.
